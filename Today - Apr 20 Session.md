### Chemistry Cheat Sheet — Electronic Effects

#### Inductive Effect
- Withdrawal or donation of electrons through sigma bonds
- **–I groups** (electron withdrawing): –F, –Cl, –Br, –I, –OH, –NO₂, –CN, –COOH
- **+I groups** (electron donating): alkyl groups (–CH₃, –C₂H₅, etc.) — more carbons = stronger +I
- Decreases with distance (almost negligible after 3 bonds)
- **Effect on acidity:** More –I groups near –COOH = more acidic (stabilizes COO⁻)
  - CCl₃COOH > CHCl₂COOH > CH₂ClCOOH > CH₃COOH
- **Effect on basicity:** More +I groups on N = more basic (alkyl amines more basic than NH₃)

#### Resonance (Mesomeric Effect)
- Delocalization of π electrons or lone pairs through conjugated system
- **+M groups** (donate electrons into ring/system): –OH, –OR, –NH₂, –NHR, –NR₂, halogens
- **–M groups** (withdraw electrons from system): –NO₂, –CN, –CHO, –COOR, –COOH
- **Key rule:** Resonance structures must have same sigma framework; only π electrons/lone pairs move
- **Phenol is more acidic than alcohol:** –OH lone pair donates into ring → phenoxide anion stabilized by resonance
- **Aniline is less basic than aliphatic amines:** N lone pair donated into ring → less available to accept H⁺

#### Hyperconjugation
- Delocalization of σ (C–H) electrons into adjacent empty p orbital or π system
- **More alkyl groups = more hyperconjugation = more stable carbocation/alkene**
- Stability of carbocations: 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃⁺
  - Reason: more C–H bonds adjacent to + charge = more hyperconjugation = more stable
- Stability of alkenes: more substituted = more stable (more hyperconjugation)
  - (CH₃)₂C=CH₂ > CH₃CH=CH₂ > CH₂=CH₂

#### Reaction Intermediates
| Intermediate | Structure | Stability Order | Formed in |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Carbocation (R⁺) | C with empty orbital, sp² | 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃⁺ | SN1, E1, addition |
| Carbanion (R⁻) | C with lone pair | 3° < 2° < 1° < CH₃⁻ (opposite!) | SN2, organometallics |
| Free radical (R•) | C with 1 unpaired e⁻ | 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃• | Halogenation of alkanes |

> **VIT exam tip:** Carbocation stability = most asked. Always: 3° > 2° > 1°. Resonance-stabilized carbocation (allylic, benzylic) > 3°.

**Set D: Mixed VIT-Style MCQs (15 min)**
13. The order of –I effect among halogens is:
    (a) F>Cl>Br>I  (b) I>Br>Cl>F  (c) Cl>F>Br>I  (d) F>I>Cl>Br

14. Which has the highest resonance energy?
    (a) Benzene  (b) Cyclohexane  (c) Cyclohexadiene  (d) Cyclohexene

15. Hyperconjugation involves:
    (a) π–π overlap  (b) σ–π overlap  (c) p–p overlap  (d) s–p overlap

16. Which carbocation is most stable?
    (a) CH₃⁺  (b) (CH₃)₂CH⁺  (c) C₆H₅CH₂⁺ (benzylic)  (d) (CH₃)₃C⁺

> [!question]- Answers — Check ONLY after attempting
> 1. CF₃COOH > CCl₃COOH > CBr₃COOH > CH₃COOH (F has strongest –I effect)
> 2. Phenol more acidic — phenoxide ion stabilized by resonance with ring; ethoxide is not
> 3. (CH₃)₂NH > CH₃NH₂ > (CH₃)₃N > NH₃ (in gas phase; in water: (CH₃)₂NH > CH₃NH₂ > NH₃ > (CH₃)₃N)
> 4. F > Cl > Br > I (decreasing –I effect), so acidity: F–CH₂COOH > Cl > Br > I
> 5. Two structures: O–H (neutral) and O⁻ (with + charge on ring at ortho/para)
> 6. Both C–O bonds have partial double bond character due to resonance delocalization
> 7. NH₂ group is +M → donates e⁻ density to o/p positions → those positions enriched → electrophile attacks there
> 8. Naphthalene (10 π electrons, more structures possible)
> 9. (CH₃)₃C⁺ > (CH₃)₂CH⁺ > CH₃CH₂⁺ > CH₃⁺
> 10. Allylic carbocation — stabilized by resonance (CH₂=CH–CH₂⁺ ↔ ⁺CH₂–CH=CH₂)
> 11. Br adds to C2 (secondary carbocation more stable than primary at C1)
> 12. 3° substrate forms stable 3° carbocation intermediate → lower activation energy
> 13. (a) F>Cl>Br>I
> 14. (a) Benzene
> 15. (b) σ–π overlap
> 16. (c) C₆H₅CH₂⁺ (benzylic — resonance stabilized by ring, more stable than even 3°)

## BLOCK 2 — Physics (9:00–11:00 PM)

### What to Read
NCERT Class 11 Physics: Chapter 3 (Motion in Straight Line) + Chapter 4 (Motion in Plane) + Chapter 5 (Laws of Motion)

### Key Formulas to Know Cold
```
v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as
s_nth = u + a(2n-1)/2

Projectile:
  Range R = u²sin2θ/g
  Max height H = u²sin²θ/2g
  Time of flight T = 2usinθ/g

Laws of Motion:
  F = ma
  Impulse = FΔt = Δp
  Friction: f = μN
```

### Physics Questions — Tonight
1. A ball is thrown at 30° with 20 m/s. Find range and max height. (g=10)
2. Two blocks of 3 kg and 5 kg connected by string on frictionless surface. Force of 16 N applied. Find acceleration and tension.
3. A car moving at 72 km/h brakes with deceleration 5 m/s². Distance to stop?
4. At what angle should a projectile be thrown to have equal range and height?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. R = (20²×sin60°)/10 = 400×(√3/2)/10 = 34.6m; H = (20²×sin²30°)/(2×10) = 400×0.25/20 = 5m
> 2. a = 16/8 = 2 m/s²; T = 5×2 = 10 N (tension acts on 5kg block)
> 3. v²=u²+2as → 0 = 20²-2×5×s → s = 400/10 = 40 m
> 4. tan θ = 4 → θ = tan⁻¹(4) ≈ 76°

## BLOCK 3 — Maths (11:00 PM–12:30 AM)

### Key Formulas
```
Standard limits:
  lim(x→0) sinx/x = 1
  lim(x→0) tanx/x = 1
  lim(x→0) (eˣ-1)/x = 1
  lim(x→0) (aˣ-1)/x = ln a
  lim(x→0) (1+x)^(1/x) = e
  lim(x→∞) (1+1/x)^x = e

Derivatives:
  d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹
  d/dx(eˣ) = eˣ
  d/dx(ln x) = 1/x
  d/dx(sin x) = cos x
  d/dx(cos x) = -sin x
  d/dx(tan x) = sec²x
```

### Maths Questions — Tonight
1. lim(x→0) (sin 3x)/(sin 5x) = ?
2. lim(x→2) (x²-4)/(x-2) = ?
3. Differentiate: x³ + 3x² + 5x + 7

5. lim(x→∞) (3x²+2x+1)/(5x²+4) = ?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. 3/5 (use sinx/x=1 for both)
