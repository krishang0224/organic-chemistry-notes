> [!question]- Who disproved the vital force theory and how?
> Friedrich Wöhler (1828) — synthesized urea (organic) from ammonium cyanate (inorganic) by heating.

> [!question]- What are the 4 special properties of carbon?
> 1. Tetravalency (4 bonds)
> 2. Catenation (C–C chains)
> 3. Multiple bonding (single, double, triple)
> 4. Small size → strong, stable bonds

> [!question]- A carbon bonded to 3 other carbons is called?
> Tertiary (3°) carbon

> [!question]- A carbon bonded to 4 other carbons and no hydrogens is called?
> Quaternary (4°) carbon

> [!question]- What is the hybridization, bond angle, and shape of methane?
> sp³, 109.5°, tetrahedral

> [!question]- What is the hybridization, bond angle, and shape of ethene?
> sp², 120°, trigonal planar

> [!question]- What is the hybridization, bond angle, and shape of ethyne?
> sp, 180°, linear

> [!question]- How many σ and π bonds are in a double bond?
> 1σ + 1π

> [!question]- How many σ and π bonds are in a triple bond?
> 1σ + 2π

> [!question]- Why can't there be free rotation around a double bond?
> Rotation would break the π bond (sideways overlap of p orbitals) — requires too much energy.

> [!question]- Which has more s-character: sp, sp², or sp³?
> sp (50% s-character)

> [!question]- Which C–H bond is most acidic: alkane, alkene, or alkyne?
> Alkyne (sp, 50% s-character holds carbanion most tightly → most stable conjugate base)

> [!question]- Arrange in increasing bond length: C≡C, C–C, C=C
> C≡C (120 pm) < C=C (134 pm) < C–C (154 pm)

> [!question]- Arrange in increasing bond energy: C–C, C=C, C≡C
> C–C (347) < C=C (614) < C≡C (839 kJ/mol)

> [!question]- General formula for alkanes?
> CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

> [!question]- General formula for alkenes (one double bond)?
> CₙH₂ₙ

> [!question]- General formula for alkynes (one triple bond)?
> CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

> [!question]- Why does benzene prefer substitution over addition reactions?
> Addition would destroy aromaticity (delocalized π system) — substitution maintains it.

> [!question]- Bond length in benzene (140 pm) is between C–C and C=C. Why?
> The 6 π electrons are fully delocalized over all 6 carbons — all bonds are equivalent, intermediate character.

> [!question]- Why can't alkanes show cis-trans isomerism?
> C–C single bonds have free rotation — any "arrangement" can interconvert freely at room temperature.

> [!question]- What is the bond angle in water (104.5°) and why is it less than methane (109.5°)?
> 2 lone pairs on O occupy more space than bonding pairs → compress H–O–H angle.

> [!question]- Energy of the π bond in C=C (approximately)?
> ~267 kJ/mol (= 614 – 347; the difference between C=C and C–C bond energies)

> [!question]- Why is C–H bond in ethyne (sp) shorter than in methane (sp³)?
> Higher s-character → electrons held closer to nucleus → shorter bond.
