# Block 2 Flashcards — Reading & Writing Compounds

> **How to use:** Read the question. Think. Then expand to reveal.

---

> [!question]- In a skeletal formula, what does each line-end and junction represent?
> One carbon atom. Hydrogens on carbon are not shown (implied to complete valency of 4).

> [!question]- Formula for Degree of Unsaturation (DoU)?
> DoU = (2C + 2 + N – H – X) / 2  (O and S don't appear in the formula)

> [!question]- What does DoU = 4 strongly suggest?
> Benzene ring (1 ring + 3 double bonds = 4 degrees of unsaturation)

> [!question]- What does DoU = 0 tell you?
> Fully saturated — no rings, no double or triple bonds (alkane or haloalkane)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC suffix for a carboxylic acid?
> -oic acid (e.g., ethanoic acid)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC suffix for an aldehyde?
> -al (e.g., ethanal, butanal)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC suffix for a ketone?
> -one (e.g., propan-2-one)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC suffix for an alcohol?
> -ol (e.g., butan-1-ol)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC suffix for an amine?
> -amine (e.g., butan-1-amine)

> [!question]- What is the IUPAC prefix for halogens?
> fluoro-, chloro-, bromo-, iodo- (used as prefixes, not suffixes)

> [!question]- Priority order for IUPAC naming (highest to lowest)?
> –COOH > –CHO > C=O > –OH > –NH₂ > C=C > C≡C > –X

> [!question]- Why is the name "3-methylbutane" wrong?
> Should be 2-methylbutane — always number from the end closest to the substituent. "3-methylbutane" uses the wrong end.

> [!question]- When two substituents are different, in what order are they listed in IUPAC name?
> Alphabetical order (ignoring multiplying prefixes like di-, tri-)

> [!question]- Multiplying prefixes for 2, 3, 4 identical substituents?
> di-, tri-, tetra-

> [!question]- Name: CH₃–CH=CH–CH₃
> But-2-ene

> [!question]- Name: HC≡C–CH₂–CH₃
> But-1-yne

> [!question]- Trivial name of ethyne?
> Acetylene

> [!question]- Trivial name of propan-2-one?
> Acetone

> [!question]- Trivial name of trichloromethane?
> Chloroform

> [!question]- What is a homologous series?
> A family of compounds with same functional group, same general formula, differing by –CH₂– between members, with gradually changing physical properties.

> [!question]- C₃H₆O has DoU = 1. Give two possible structures.
> Propanal (CH₃CH₂CHO) — aldehyde; Propan-2-one (CH₃COCH₃) — ketone. These are functional group isomers.

> [!question]- Why does pentan-1-ol have a much higher boiling point than pentane (same carbon count)?
> Pentan-1-ol has –OH group → intermolecular hydrogen bonding → much more energy needed to separate molecules.

> [!question]- Acidity order: phenol, ethanol, ethanoic acid?
> Ethanoic acid > phenol > ethanol (pKa: ~5 < ~10 < ~16)

> [!question]- What is functional group isomerism?
> Same molecular formula but different functional groups. Example: C₃H₆O → propanal (aldehyde) vs propanone (ketone).

> [!question]- Chain prefixes: 7, 8, 9, 10 carbons?
> hept-, oct-, non-, dec-
