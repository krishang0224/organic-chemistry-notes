## Preview
- What is a nucleophile vs electrophile
- SN1: 2-step, carbocation intermediate, racemization, favored by 3°
- SN2: 1-step, backside attack, inversion of configuration (Walden inversion), favored by 1°
- Comparison table: substrate, nucleophile, solvent, stereochemistry
- Competing reactions: SN1 vs E1, SN2 vs E2
- Nucleophilicity vs basicity
- Leaving group ability order
