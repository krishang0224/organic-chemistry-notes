# 3.4 Reaction Intermediates

> [!tip] One Line
> Carbocations, carbanions, and free radicals are short-lived species formed during reactions — their stability determines which product forms.

---

## Carbocations (R⁺)

- Carbon with **empty p orbital**, sp² hybridized, planar
- **Stability:** 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃⁺
- Extra stable: **benzylic** (C₆H₅CH₂⁺) and **allylic** (CH₂=CHCH₂⁺) — resonance stabilized
- Extra unstable: **vinyl** (CH₂=CH⁺) and **phenyl** (C₆H₅⁺) — cannot be resonance stabilized
- Formed in: SN1, E1, electrophilic addition to alkenes

## Carbanions (R⁻)

- Carbon with **lone pair**, sp³ hybridized
- **Stability: opposite of carbocations** — more s-character = more stable
  - sp carbanion (RC≡C⁻) > sp² > sp³
  - 1° > 2° > 3° (alkyl groups destabilize by +I)
- Stabilized by –I groups (–NO₂, –CN, halogens) nearby
- Formed in: organometallic reactions, base-catalyzed reactions

## Free Radicals (R•)

- Carbon with **one unpaired electron**, sp² hybridized, planar
- **Stability:** 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃• (same as carbocations — hyperconjugation)
- Benzylic and allylic radicals are extra stable (resonance)
- Formed in: halogenation of alkanes (free radical mechanism), polymerization

## Comparison Table

| Feature | Carbocation | Carbanion | Free Radical |
|---------|------------|-----------|--------------|
| Electrons on C | 0 (empty orbital) | 2 (lone pair) | 1 (unpaired) |
| Hybridization | sp² | sp³ | sp² |
| Shape | Planar | Pyramidal | Planar |
| Stability order | 3°>2°>1° | 1°>2°>3° | 3°>2°>1° |
| Formed in | SN1, addition | Organometallics | Free radical rxns |

## Practice Questions

1. Which is more stable: (CH₃)₃C⁺ or C₆H₅CH₂⁺ (benzylic)?
2. Why is CH₃⁻ more stable than (CH₃)₃C⁻?
3. Arrange free radicals: CH₃•, (CH₃)₂CH•, (CH₃)₃C•
4. Why does Cl₂ react with methane by free radical mechanism and not ionic?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. C₆H₅CH₂⁺ — resonance stabilization by benzene ring is stronger than hyperconjugation alone
> 2. Alkyl groups have +I effect — they push electrons onto carbanion, destabilizing it. CH₃⁻ has no such destabilization
> 3. (CH₃)₃C• > (CH₃)₂CH• > CH₃•
> 4. Methane has no π bond and no good leaving group — ionic pathway not feasible. C–H bond breaks homolytically under UV/heat giving radicals
