# 4.4 Benzene & Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution (EAS)

> [!tip] One Line
> Benzene is aromatic — it substitutes rather than adds to preserve its stable delocalized π system.

---

## Aromaticity — Hückel's Rule
A compound is aromatic if it is: cyclic + planar + fully conjugated + has **(4n+2) π electrons**

| n | π electrons | Example |
|---|------------|---------|
| 0 | 2 | — |
| 1 | 6 | Benzene ✅ |
| 2 | 10 | Naphthalene ✅ |

Benzene: 6 π electrons, all bonds equal (140 pm), extra stable (resonance energy = 150 kJ/mol)

---

## EAS General Mechanism
```
Step 1: Electrophile (E⁺) attacks ring → arenium ion (sigma complex, carbocation)
Step 2: Base removes H⁺ → aromaticity restored
```
The ring acts as nucleophile. Electrophile adds, then H is lost (not added) — preserves aromaticity.

---

## EAS Reactions

### Halogenation (Cl₂ or Br₂ / Lewis acid catalyst)
```
C₆H₆ + Cl₂ → C₆H₅Cl + HCl    (catalyst: FeCl₃ or AlCl₃)
```
FeCl₃ activates Cl₂ by forming Cl⁺ (electrophile)

### Nitration
```
C₆H₆ + HNO₃ → C₆H₅NO₂ + H₂O    (conc. H₂SO₄ catalyst, 55°C)
```
H₂SO₄ generates NO₂⁺ (nitronium ion) — the electrophile

### Sulfonation
```
C₆H₆ + H₂SO₄ → C₆H₅SO₃H + H₂O    (fuming H₂SO₄, reversible)
```

### Friedel-Crafts Alkylation
```
C₆H₆ + RCl → C₆H₅R + HCl    (AlCl₃ catalyst)
```
Limitation: multiple alkylation, carbocation rearrangement

### Friedel-Crafts Acylation
```
C₆H₆ + RCOCl → C₆H₅COR + HCl    (AlCl₃ catalyst)
```
No rearrangement (acylium ion R–C≡O⁺ is stable). Preferred over alkylation.

---

## Directing Effects

### ortho/para Directors (+M or +I groups)
Activate ring, direct E⁺ to ortho and para positions.
**Groups:** –OH, –OR, –NH₂, –NHR, –CH₃, alkyl groups, –X (halogens — deactivate but o/p direct)

### meta Directors (–M groups)
Deactivate ring, direct E⁺ to meta position.
**Groups:** –NO₂, –CN, –CHO, –COR, –COOH, –SO₃H, –NR₃⁺

> [!important] Halogens are DEACTIVATORS but ortho/para directors — exception to remember.

## Practice Questions
1. Write the product of benzene + Br₂/FeBr₃
2. Why does benzene undergo substitution not addition?
3. –NO₂ group directs incoming electrophile to which position?
4. Product of toluene (methylbenzene) + HNO₃/H₂SO₄?
5. Why is Friedel-Crafts acylation preferred over alkylation?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. Bromobenzene (C₆H₅Br) + HBr
> 2. Addition would destroy aromaticity (lose 150 kJ/mol resonance energy); substitution preserves it
> 3. meta position (–NO₂ is –M, meta director)
> 4. Mixture of o-nitrotoluene and p-nitrotoluene (–CH₃ is o/p director)
> 5. Acylium ion doesn't rearrange; alkylation can give rearranged products and multiple substitutions
