# 4.4 Benzene & Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution (EAS)

> [!warning] Note
> This note will be filled in detail when we reach Block 4.

## Preview
- Aromaticity: Hückel's rule (4n+2 π electrons)
- Why benzene prefers substitution over addition
- EAS mechanism: formation of arenium ion (sigma complex)
- Reactions: halogenation, nitration, sulfonation, Friedel-Crafts alkylation/acylation
- Directing effects: ortho/para directors vs meta directors
- Activation and deactivation of ring
- Connection to phenol and aniline (Class 12)
