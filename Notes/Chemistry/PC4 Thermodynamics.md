# PC4 Thermodynamics

> [!tip] One Line
> ΔG = ΔH – TΔS decides spontaneity. ΔG < 0: spontaneous. First law: energy conserved. Second law: entropy increases in universe.

---

## First Law of Thermodynamics

*Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted.*

```
ΔU = q + w    (q = heat absorbed by system, w = work done ON system)
w = –PΔV      (for expansion against constant pressure)
```

At constant pressure: **q = ΔH** (enthalpy change)
At constant volume: **q = ΔU**

**ΔH = ΔU + ΔngRT**
where Δng = moles of gaseous products – moles of gaseous reactants

---

## Enthalpy

**Standard enthalpy of formation (ΔfH°):** enthalpy change to form 1 mol compound from elements in standard states. For elements in standard state: ΔfH° = 0.

**Hess's Law:** ΔH for a reaction = sum of ΔH of steps (path-independent).

**Bond enthalpy method:**
```
ΔH_rxn = Σ(bond energies of bonds broken) – Σ(bond energies of bonds formed)
```
Endothermic if more energy absorbed in breaking than released in forming.

**Types of enthalpy:**
| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| ΔcombH | Enthalpy of combustion (always exothermic) |
| ΔfusH | Enthalpy of fusion (solid → liquid) |
| ΔvapH | Enthalpy of vaporization (liquid → gas) |
| ΔsolH | Enthalpy of solution (dissolving) |
| ΔneutH | Enthalpy of neutralization (strong acid + strong base = –57.1 kJ/mol always) |

---

## Second Law & Entropy

*Entropy (disorder) of the universe always increases in spontaneous processes.*

**ΔSuniverse = ΔSsystem + ΔSsurroundings > 0** (for spontaneous process)

**Entropy increases when:**
- Solid → liquid → gas
- Dissolving solids/liquids in water
- Number of moles of gas increases
- Temperature increases

---

## Gibbs Free Energy

```
ΔG = ΔH – TΔS    (at constant T and P)
ΔG < 0: spontaneous
ΔG = 0: equilibrium
ΔG > 0: non-spontaneous (reverse is spontaneous)
```

| ΔH | ΔS | ΔG = ΔH – TΔS | Spontaneous? |
|----|-----|----------------|-------------|
| – | + | Always – | Always spontaneous |
| + | – | Always + | Never spontaneous |
| – | – | – at low T | Spontaneous at low T |
| + | + | – at high T | Spontaneous at high T |

**Standard Gibbs energy:**
```
ΔG° = –RT ln K = –nFE°cell
ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q
```

---

## Heat Capacities

```
Cp – Cv = R    (for ideal gas; R = 8.314 J/mol·K)
```
- Cp: heat capacity at constant pressure
- Cv: heat capacity at constant volume
- For monatomic gas: Cv = 3/2 R, Cp = 5/2 R
- For diatomic gas: Cv = 5/2 R, Cp = 7/2 R

---

## Kirchhoff's Law
ΔH changes with temperature:
```
ΔH₂ = ΔH₁ + ΔCp(T₂ – T₁)
```

## Practice Questions
1. If ΔH < 0 and ΔS < 0, at what temperature is reaction spontaneous?
2. For water boiling at 100°C, ΔG = ?
3. ΔG° = –57.3 kJ/mol at 298 K. Find K.
4. Which has higher entropy: 1 mol NaCl(s) or 1 mol NaCl(aq)?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. Low temperature (ΔG = ΔH – TΔS; both negative, so ΔG < 0 when T is small enough that |ΔH| > T|ΔS|)
> 2. ΔG = 0 (system at equilibrium — boiling point is equilibrium between liquid and gas)
> 3. –57300 = –8.314 × 298 × ln K → ln K = 23.12 → K ≈ e²³ ≈ 1.1 × 10¹⁰
> 4. NaCl(aq) — ions dispersed in solution have more disorder than ordered crystal lattice
