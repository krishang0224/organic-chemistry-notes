# 3.2 Resonance (Mesomeric Effect)

> [!tip] One Line
> Delocalization of π electrons or lone pairs over a conjugated system — the molecule is a hybrid of all resonance structures, not a mixture.

---

## What Resonance Is (and Isn't)

- **Is:** A way of representing electron delocalization using multiple structures
- **Is NOT:** The molecule switching between structures — it's one single hybrid
- **Only π electrons and lone pairs move** — sigma framework stays fixed

---

## Rules for Drawing Resonance Structures
1. Only electrons move, never atoms
2. Total charge must be conserved
3. Octet rule should not be violated (except for expanded octets in period 3+)
4. Lone pairs adjacent to π bonds or empty orbitals can delocalize

---

## +M and –M Groups

### +M (Electron Donating by Resonance)
Donate lone pair into π system: **–OH, –OR, –NH₂, –NHR, –NR₂, halogens (–F, –Cl, –Br, –I)**

> Halogens are –I but +M — they withdraw by induction but donate by resonance

### –M (Electron Withdrawing by Resonance)
Pull electrons from π system: **–NO₂, –CN, –CHO, –COR, –COOR, –COOH**

---

## Key Applications

### Phenol more acidic than alcohol
Phenoxide ion (C₆H₅O⁻) is stabilized by resonance — negative charge delocalized into ring.
Ethoxide (C₂H₅O⁻) has no resonance stabilization.

### Aniline less basic than aliphatic amines
N lone pair is delocalized into benzene ring → less available to accept H⁺

### Carboxylate ion stability
Both C–O bonds in COO⁻ are equivalent (140 pm) — charge delocalized over both oxygens

### Benzylic/Allylic carbocations are extra stable
CH₂=CH–CH₂⁺ ↔ ⁺CH₂–CH=CH₂ (allylic — resonance stabilized)
C₆H₅–CH₂⁺ (benzylic — stabilized by ring)

---

## Practice Questions

1. Draw 2 resonance structures of the carboxylate ion (RCOO⁻)
2. Why are both C–O bonds in RCOO⁻ equal length?
3. Why does –NH₂ group direct electrophiles to ortho/para in aniline?
4. Arrange in acidity: phenol, ethanol, ethanoic acid
5. Which is more stable: CH₂=CH–CH₂⁺ or CH₃–CH₂–CH₂⁺?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. One with C=O and C–O⁻, another with C–O⁻ and C=O (swapped)
> 2. Both bonds have equal partial double bond character due to delocalization
> 3. +M effect of –NH₂ pushes e⁻ density to o/p positions → electrophile attacks there
> 4. Ethanoic acid (pKa~5) > Phenol (pKa~10) > Ethanol (pKa~16)
> 5. CH₂=CH–CH₂⁺ (allylic, resonance stabilized)
