# 4.1 Alkanes

> [!tip] One Line
> Saturated hydrocarbons — only C–C and C–H single bonds. Relatively unreactive, undergo substitution not addition.

---

## Physical Properties
- BP increases with chain length (more surface area = more van der Waals)
- Branched isomers have lower BP than straight chain (less surface contact)
- Insoluble in water, soluble in organic solvents
- Less dense than water

## Free Radical Halogenation

**Overall:** CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl (in presence of UV light or heat)

**Mechanism:**
```
Initiation:    Cl₂ → 2Cl•           (homolytic cleavage by UV)

Propagation:   Cl• + CH₄ → HCl + CH₃•
               CH₃• + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + Cl•   (chain reaction)

Termination:   Cl• + Cl• → Cl₂
               CH₃• + Cl• → CH₃Cl
               CH₃• + CH₃• → C₂H₆
```

**Reactivity of halogens:** F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂
- F₂: too reactive (explosive)
- I₂: too unreactive (reaction reversible)
- Cl₂ and Br₂: practical

**Selectivity:** Br• is more selective than Cl•
- Br• attacks 3° H preferentially (more stable radical intermediate)
- Cl• attacks all H's more randomly

**Reactivity of H atoms:** 3° H > 2° H > 1° H (easier to abstract)

## Combustion
```
CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O  (complete)
2CH₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO + 4H₂O  (incomplete — CO formed)
```

## Practice Questions
1. Write mechanism for chlorination of ethane
2. Why does Br₂ show more selectivity than Cl₂ in halogenation?
3. Major product of bromination of 2-methylpropane?
4. Why is F₂ not used directly for fluorination of alkanes?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. Initiation: Cl₂→2Cl•; Propagation: Cl•+C₂H₆→HCl+C₂H₅•, C₂H₅•+Cl₂→C₂H₅Cl+Cl•
> 2. Br• is less reactive → more selective → preferentially abstracts more stable (3°) H
> 3. 2-bromo-2-methylpropane (Br attacks 3° carbon — most stable radical)
> 4. F₂ is too reactive — reaction is explosive and uncontrollable
