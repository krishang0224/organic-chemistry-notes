# PC1 Mole Concept & Stoichiometry

> [!tip] One Line
> 1 mole = 6.022×10²³ particles = molar mass in grams = 22.4 L gas at STP. Everything flows from this.

---

## Core Definitions

| Quantity | Formula | Units |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Moles (n) | n = mass/M | mol |
| Molar mass (M) | sum of atomic masses | g/mol |
| Avogadro's number | Nₐ = 6.022 × 10²³ | mol⁻¹ |
| Volume at STP (0°C, 1 atm) | V = n × 22.4 | L |
| Number of particles | N = n × Nₐ | — |

---

## Empirical & Molecular Formula

**Empirical formula:** simplest whole-number ratio of atoms
**Molecular formula = (empirical formula) × n**, where n = M / empirical formula mass

**Steps to find empirical formula:**
1. Convert % composition to grams (assume 100 g)
2. Divide by atomic mass → molar ratio
3. Divide by smallest → whole number ratio

---

## Concentration Terms

| Term | Formula | Units |
|------|---------|-------|
| Molarity (M) | moles of solute / L of solution | mol/L |
| Molality (m) | moles of solute / kg of solvent | mol/kg |
| Mole fraction (χ) | nₐ / (nₐ + n_b) | dimensionless |
| Normality (N) | equivalents / L | eq/L |
| % by mass | (mass solute / mass solution) × 100 | % |

**Key:** Molality doesn't change with temperature; molarity does (volume changes).

**Dilution:** M₁V₁ = M₂V₂ (for same solute, moles conserved)

---

## Stoichiometry

### Mole Ratio
From balanced equation: coefficients = mole ratios
```
N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃
1 mol N₂ : 3 mol H₂ : 2 mol NH₃
```

### Limiting Reagent
- Reagent that runs out first → determines product amount
- Find moles of each reactant, check which limits based on stoichiometry
- **% yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100**

---

## Equivalent Concept

**Equivalent weight:**
- Acid: M / basicity (no. of H⁺ donated)
- Base: M / acidity (no. of OH⁻ donated)
- Oxidizing/reducing agent: M / change in oxidation state per molecule

**N = M × n-factor** (where n-factor = valence factor)

At equivalence point: **N₁V₁ = N₂V₂**

---

## Oxidation State Rules (quick)
1. Free element = 0
2. Monatomic ion = charge
3. O = –2 (except in peroxides: –1, OF₂: +2)
4. H = +1 (except in metal hydrides: –1)
5. Sum = charge of species

---

## Important Values to Remember
- Atomic mass: H=1, C=12, N=14, O=16, Na=23, Cl=35.5, Fe=56, Cu=63.5
- 1 L = 1000 mL = 1000 cm³
- STP: 0°C, 1 atm (old); IUPAC STP: 0°C, 100 kPa → 22.7 L/mol (but most problems use 22.4)

## Practice Questions
1. Find moles in 9 g of H₂O
2. 2 mol Na₂CO₃ dissolved in 500 mL → Molarity?
3. In N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃: if 28 g N₂ and 9 g H₂ react, which is limiting?
4. Equivalent weight of H₂SO₄?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. 9/18 = 0.5 mol
> 2. M = 2/0.5 = 4 M
> 3. 28 g N₂ = 1 mol, needs 3 mol H₂; 9 g H₂ = 4.5 mol available. 1 mol N₂ needs 3 mol H₂, only 3 mol used → H₂ is in excess, N₂ is limiting (wait: 4.5 mol H₂ > 3 mol needed → N₂ is limiting)
> 4. M/2 = 98/2 = 49 g/eq (H₂SO₄ donates 2H⁺, basicity = 2)
