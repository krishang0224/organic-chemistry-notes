# 6.1 Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers

> [!tip] One Line
> –OH on carbon = alcohol; –OH on benzene = phenol (more acidic); –O– between two carbons = ether (unreactive).

---

## Alcohols

### Classification
- 1° alcohol: –OH on carbon bonded to 1 other carbon (e.g., ethanol)
- 2° alcohol: –OH on carbon bonded to 2 carbons (e.g., propan-2-ol)
- 3° alcohol: –OH on carbon bonded to 3 carbons (e.g., 2-methylpropan-2-ol)

### Key Reactions
**1. With Na metal:** 2ROH + 2Na → 2RONa + H₂↑ (all alcohols react; phenol reacts faster)

**2. Lucas Test (ZnCl₂/conc. HCl):**
- 3° alcohol: turbidity immediately
- 2° alcohol: turbidity in 5 min
- 1° alcohol: no turbidity (or very slow)

**3. Oxidation:**
```
1° alcohol → aldehyde → carboxylic acid   (KMnO₄ or K₂Cr₂O₇/H₂SO₄)
2° alcohol → ketone                        (cannot be oxidised further easily)
3° alcohol → no oxidation (no H on C–OH)
```

**4. Dehydration (conc. H₂SO₄):**
```
At 170°C: alcohol → alkene (elimination)
At 130°C: alcohol → ether (substitution, 2 molecules)
```

**5. Esterification:** ROH + RCOOH ⇌ RCOOR + H₂O (H₂SO₄ catalyst, reversible)

---

## Phenols

### Why More Acidic than Alcohols
Phenoxide ion (C₆H₅O⁻) stabilized by resonance with ring → pKa ≈ 10
Ethoxide (C₂H₅O⁻) no resonance → pKa ≈ 16

### Acidity order
p-nitrophenol > phenol > p-methylphenol (–NO₂ withdraws e⁻, stabilizes phenoxide more)

### Key Reactions
**1. Reimer-Tiemann Reaction:** Phenol + CHCl₃/NaOH → salicylaldehyde (2-hydroxybenzaldehyde)

**2. Kolbe Reaction:** Phenol + CO₂/NaOH → salicylic acid (2-hydroxybenzoic acid)

**3. Bromination:** Phenol + Br₂(aq) → 2,4,6-tribromophenol↓ (white ppt, no catalyst needed — –OH activates ring strongly)

---

## Ethers (R–O–R')

- Generally unreactive
- **Cleavage by HI (excess):** R–O–R' + 2HI → 2RI + H₂O
- Used as solvents (diethyl ether, THF)
- **Williamson synthesis:** RONa + R'X → R–O–R' + NaX (best method, SN2)

---

## Practice Questions
1. Arrange in acidity: ethanol, phenol, p-nitrophenol
2. Lucas test: which alcohol reacts immediately?
3. Product of ethanol + conc. H₂SO₄ at 170°C?
4. How is salicylic acid prepared from phenol?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. p-nitrophenol > phenol > ethanol
> 2. 3° alcohol (turbidity immediate)
> 3. Ethylene (CH₂=CH₂) — dehydration/elimination
> 4. Kolbe reaction: phenol + CO₂ under pressure with NaOH → sodium salicylate → acidify → salicylic acid
