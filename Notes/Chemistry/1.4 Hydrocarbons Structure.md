# 1.4 Hydrocarbons — Structure

> [!tip] One Line
> Hydrocarbons = compounds of only C and H. Their structure directly follows from hybridization.

---

## Classification

```
Hydrocarbons
├── Aliphatic (open chain)
│   ├── Alkanes (only single bonds) — sp³
│   ├── Alkenes (one or more C=C) — sp²
│   └── Alkynes (one or more C≡C) — sp
└── Aromatic (benzene ring)
    └── Benzene and derivatives — sp²
```

---

## Alkanes — sp³

**General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂**

| Name | Formula | Structure |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Methane | CH₄ | C with 4 H |
| Ethane | C₂H₆ | CH₃–CH₃ |
| Propane | C₃H₈ | CH₃–CH₂–CH₃ |
| Butane | C₄H₁₀ | CH₃–(CH₂)₂–CH₃ |
| Pentane | C₅H₁₂ | CH₃–(CH₂)₃–CH₃ |

- All carbons: **sp³ hybridized** → tetrahedral, 109.5°
- All bonds: **single bonds (σ only)**
- Free rotation around C–C bonds
- Saturated (no more H can be added)
- Relatively unreactive (strong σ bonds, no π bonds to attack)

---

## Alkenes — sp²

**General formula: CₙH₂ₙ** (one double bond)

| Name | Formula | Structure |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Ethene | C₂H₄ | CH₂=CH₂ |
| Propene | C₃H₆ | CH₂=CH–CH₃ |
| But-1-ene | C₄H₈ | CH₂=CH–CH₂–CH₃ |
| But-2-ene | C₄H₈ | CH₃–CH=CH–CH₃ |

- Double bond carbons: **sp²**, rest are sp³
- Double bond carbons + attached atoms: all in **one plane**
- Bond angle at double bond: **120°**
- **No free rotation** at C=C (π bond prevents it)
- Unsaturated — can undergo addition reactions

### Why Cis-Trans Isomerism Occurs in Alkenes
```
    H    H          H    CH₃
     \  /            \  /
      C=C             C=C
     /  \            /  \
  CH₃   CH₃        H    H
  
  cis-but-2-ene    trans-but-2-ene
  (same side)      (opposite sides)
```
Possible ONLY because double bond has no free rotation.

---

## Alkynes — sp

**General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂** (one triple bond)

| Name | Formula | Structure |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Ethyne | C₂H₂ | HC≡CH |
| Propyne | C₃H₄ | HC≡C–CH₃ |
| But-1-yne | C₄H₆ | HC≡C–CH₂–CH₃ |
| But-2-yne | C₄H₆ | CH₃–C≡C–CH₃ |

- Triple bond carbons: **sp hybridized**
- Geometry: **linear** (180°)
- Most unsaturated among the three
- Terminal alkynes (HC≡C–) have **acidic H** (can be removed by strong base)

---

## Benzene — Aromatic

**Molecular formula: C₆H₆**

- 6 carbons in a ring, each **sp²**
- 3 double bonds alternating with 3 single bonds (but actually all bonds are equivalent — delocalization)
- All 6 carbons and 6 hydrogens in **one plane**
- Bond angle: **120°**
- Bond length in benzene: **140 pm** (between C–C 154 pm and C=C 134 pm — because delocalized)

```
    H       H
     \     /
  H — C   C — H
      ||   |
  H — C   C — H
     /     \
    H       H

  Or drawn as:  ⬡  (hexagon with circle inside)
```

> [!important]
> Benzene does NOT undergo addition reactions like alkenes — it prefers **substitution** (EAS) to maintain aromaticity. This is covered in Block 4.

## Quick Comparison

| Property | Alkane | Alkene | Alkyne | Benzene |
|----------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| General formula | CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ | CₙH₂ₙ | CₙH₂ₙ₋₂ | C₆H₆ |
| Hybridization | sp³ | sp² (at C=C) | sp (at C≡C) | sp² |
| Bond angle | 109.5° | 120° | 180° | 120° |
| Typical reaction | Substitution | Addition | Addition | Substitution (EAS) |
| Saturation | Saturated | Unsaturated | Most unsaturated | Special (aromatic) |

## Practice Questions

1. What is the hybridization of each carbon in CH₃–CH=CH–C≡CH?

2. Draw cis and trans isomers of but-2-ene. Why don't alkanes show cis-trans isomerism?

3. General formula for alkyne with 5 carbons?

4. Why is benzene's C–C bond length (140 pm) between C–C and C=C bond lengths?

5. How many σ bonds and π bonds in benzene (C₆H₆)?

> [!question]- Answers
> 1. C1(sp³)–C2(sp²)=C3(sp²)–C4(sp)≡C5(sp)
> 2. Alkanes have free rotation around single bonds so any "cis" and "trans" arrangement can interconvert freely
> 3. C₅H₈ (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, n=5)
> 4. The 6 π electrons are delocalized over all 6 carbons — all bonds are equivalent, intermediate between single and double
> 5. σ bonds: 6 (C-C) + 6 (C-H) = 12; π bonds: 3 (the 3 double bonds, but delocalized) = 3
